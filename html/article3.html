SK Hynix Presents 16 GB DDR5 RAM Modules

Author: Marek Czerniejewski | 20/11/2018

Is this the beginning of the end of DDR4? It seems like only yesterday DDR4 was the next big thing and DDR3 slowly faded away into the void of outdated technology to be forgotten forever. Soon enough DDR4, the current gen RAM technology will face the same fate, as do all things in the tech world.

In comes SK Hynix… They have produced the first DDR5 modules that are in compliance with the JEDEC regulations. The modules are produced using a 10 nm architecture, that is, the second generation, revised 10 nm architecture. These modules will form the blueprint on which all future DDR5 RAM sticks will be based, and the ones that will replace DDR4.

Due to the smaller manufacturing process, more memory can be squeezed in on one module, which means 16 GB will be the new 8 GB. Operating voltage has also been decreased from 1.2V down to 1.1V. This coupled with various optimizations allows for a module that is 30% more energy efficient than current DDR4 modules. Transfer of data has increased by 60%, up to 5200 Mb/s.

SK Hynix is projecting mass production to start by 2020. At first DDR5 will be available mostly to corporations but, by the company’s estimates it will soon take the tech world by storm, with 44% of all RAM orders being DDR5 by 2022.
